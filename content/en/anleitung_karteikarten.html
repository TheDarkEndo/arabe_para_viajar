How to print your index cards
 
Choose the corresponding print version by clicking on the button
  • The header "Both sides" contains index cards that when printed will show both the foreign word and its English translation on the front side of the card. It is best to print the list on thin carton. Most printers will be able to print on it. The advantage is that when you study, the back side will not shine through.

  • If you choose "Front only" from the menu you will only print the front side containing the foreign words.
  • You should now proceed by taking the pile of paper you have just printed on. Turn it over so that the blank side is facing up. Now, insert the whole pile into the printer turning them upside down If you choose "Back only" from the menu your computer will print the translations matching them to the front.
    You can easily cut out the cards using scissors or a cardboard cutter.

Learn even more effectively

Each time you get a word from your pile of cards right first time, take a pen and mark a dot on the card. When you have accumulated five dots on a card this means that the word has passed into your long-term memory. In that case, put the card away and replace it by a new one. How do I print the list?

Start by clicking on any part of text within the index card frame with your left mouse button to ensure that the computer will print the correct part of the page. You should now print the page by clicking on the printer icon of the browser task bar. Alternatively you can choose the print command in the browser menu.

You can also copy the content of the browser page into any word processing application (e.g. Word, OpenOffice etc.) where you can edit and print it.
Just follow these steps:
1. Use your mouse to select everything you would like to copy. (By pressing CTRL+A you will select the entire content of the window)
2. Press CTRL+C to copy the content. Alternatively you may click your right mouse button and select COPY from the pop-up menu bar.
3. Switch to your word processing application (e.g. Word, OpenOffice)
4. Press CTRL+V to paste the selected parts into the word processing document. It is also possible to use the PASTE command in the taskbar.
